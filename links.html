Links

Herein lie portals to stupendously awesome websites I find useful.

News

  • Slashdot

    "News for nerds, stuff that matters".

  • Hackaday

    Viz, articles on crazy engineering projects. Among other things.

  • Hacker News

    A mostly-software-related news aggregator.

  • The New York Times

    The canonical world news source.

Reference

  • Wikipedia

    As close as it gets to a universal compendium of human knowledge. Or to the Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy.

  • Hyperphysics

    Like Wikipedia, but simpler and easier to digest. Physics only (and a little math).

  • Engineering Toolbox

    Reference tables and conversions. All of them.

  • The On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (OEIS)

    Ever wonder where the sequence 4, 6, 9, 10, 14, 15... comes from? OEIS has your back. See A001358.

Tools

  • GitHub

    The trendy way to manage software source code.

Vendors

  • McMaster-Carr

    The hardware vendor.

  • Sparkfun

    Parts for DIY-style electronic projects. The selection can be small and overpriced, but the documentation is excellent.

  • Adafruit

    More of the same.

  • Pololu

    Everything one needs for small-scale robotics.

  • Digikey

    Everything one needs for any scale electronics.

  • Mouser

    Pretty much another Digikey.

Libraries

  • Project Gutenberg

    All the public domain ebooks you'll ever need. Well, most of the old ones, anyway.

  • IMSLP

    Project Gutenberg for sheet music.

  • Pixabay

    Free public domain images FTW!
