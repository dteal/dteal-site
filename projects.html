Projects

I've build a few things over time, some of which I've listed here in chronological order.

Energy Storage Demo Rig

Fall 2015 - Summer 2016

This model demonstrates pumped-hydroelectric energy storage, i.e., power storage via gravitational potential energy of water. Built for UT Austin's fall 2015 Freshman Introduction to Research in Engineering (FIRE) course.

Battletank Mesa

Summer 2016

So I didn't have to reroute fifty wires every time I moved my computer setup, I bolted everything into a hunk of industrial unistrut. I appreciate the easy access to USB and power ports in front. Um, I name my computers after landforms, and this thing has survived falling three feet to the floor too often to be called anything but a tank.

Piano

Summer 2016

Pi-ano, if you prefer. I attached a monitor, keyboard, mouse, and Raspberry Pi to my digital keyboard to remove the need for physical sheet music storage.

Lasered Chess

Spring 2016

It turns out that glass laser etches well, so I made a chessboard from a piece I had lying about. Created with tools from the Longhorn Maker Studio.

Freedom to Make

Spring 2016

The UT Cockrell School of Engineering hosts a yearly contest for which one builds a statue of the Cockrell mascot, Alec. I created this lasercut cardbord form for the UT Longhorn Maker Studio Club.

MARKOVI

Spring 2016

Here's a robot originally built for the 2016 RAS Convention for Unconventional Robotic Movement (CURM). I'm still tweaking it.

Ultron

Fall 2015

This robot I created for the UT RAS 2015 Robotathon competition was pretty much a wooden Roomba that vacuumed ping-pong balls and marbles. Was awarded best design after placing first.

GiraPHPHe

Fall 2014 - Spring 2015

My high school robotics team's robot for the 2015-2016 First Tech Challenge and final resting place of way too much of my senior year's spare time. After leading the design and build of this thing, I helped take it to the internation championship, where we placed second.

FERmi 14

Spring 2014

My 2014 robotics class final exam competition entry. This was a human controlled, remotely powered, and double sided sumobot. Placed first, too.

POV Globe

Spring 2014

What happens when you put an Arduino and a bunch of blinky LEDs on a 300 RPM computer fan?
